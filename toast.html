Clifton café introduces £9 'artisan' toast – and locals are absolutely furious

The hand-crafted slice comes with 'foraged' butter and a side of outrage from residents who say it's gone too far

By What's On Reporter · 09:40, 17 JUN 2026

A café in Clifton has sparked fury after adding a single slice of “artisan” toast to its menu for the eye-watering price of £9.

The slice, described on the menu as “hand-torn sourdough finished with cultured foraged butter and a whisper of Cornish sea salt”, has divided opinion across the city.

Supporters say the bread is made over a 48-hour fermentation and is “worth every penny”. Critics say it is, fundamentally, toast.

One regular said she “nearly fell off the stool” when she saw the price, adding that her nan “made better for about 4p”.

The café's owner defended the dish, insisting the cost reflects the craft involved.

“It's not just toast. It's a journey. The bread is older than some relationships.”
