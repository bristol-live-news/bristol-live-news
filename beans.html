M5 traffic chaos near Avonmouth after lorry sheds entire load of baked beans

Drivers faced long delays as recovery crews tackled what one officer called 'the sauciest spillage we've seen in years'

By Traffic & Travel Reporter · 08:15, 17 JUN 2026

Motorists faced lengthy delays on the M5 near Avonmouth this morning after a lorry shed its entire load of baked beans across two lanes of the northbound carriageway.

The incident, which happened shortly after 7am near junction 18, left the road surface coated in what witnesses described as “an absolute lake of orange”.

National Highways closed two lanes while specialist recovery crews worked to clear the spillage, with tailbacks reaching several miles at the height of rush hour.

One driver stuck in the queue said the smell was “genuinely overwhelming” and that several cars had given up and turned around.

A recovery officer at the scene was philosophical about the clean-up operation.

“Forty thousand tins. It's the sauciest spillage we've seen in years – the lads are not best pleased.”
