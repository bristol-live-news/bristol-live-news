'Mystery' Banksy appears overnight in Bedminster car park – but locals aren't convinced

The stencilled artwork drew crowds within hours, though several residents say they have 'a fair idea' who's behind it

By Arts & Culture Reporter · 10:20, 17 JUN 2026

A “mystery” new artwork bearing all the hallmarks of Banksy has appeared overnight on the wall of a Bedminster car park, drawing crowds within hours.

The stencilled piece – depicting a traffic warden releasing a balloon shaped like a parking ticket – was discovered by a dog walker early on Wednesday morning.

Within an hour, a small crowd had gathered, several holding up phones and at least one selling teas from a flask.

While many were quick to hail it as a genuine Banksy, not everyone in the famously art-savvy neighbourhood was convinced of its mysterious origins.

One local, sipping a coffee nearby, raised an eyebrow at the timing.

“Mystery artist? It went up the same week the council announced new parking charges. You do the maths.”
